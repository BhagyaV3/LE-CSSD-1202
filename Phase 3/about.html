About Us

What is an Ecological Footprint?

The Ecological Footprint is a model used to quantify the demands on and supply of natural resources used by people ranging from individuals to larger communities. The demands are quantified by the sum of all the biologically productive areas with their resources that the person(s) in question requires, and the supplies by the biocapacities of those areas. The Ecological Footprint should not be confused with the Carbon Footprint, which only concerns total carbon emissions, since it involves a lot more natural resources.

What is ECO-Foottracker?

ECO-Foottracker is a website where users can track and optimize their ecological footprints for free, therefore practicing environmental friendliness and helping the world! After signing up, you would input your data into each of the buttons on our Foottracker (in Tracker), which concerns these major resources/activities: carbon emissions, electricity, transportation, food, waste, and water.
